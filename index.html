About Me

Hi! My name is Luc Tourangeau. I am an aspiring software developer with a background in just about everything.

I earned my BS in Physics from Pennsylvania State University in 2019. Immediately after graduation, I began to work in academia as a research-assistant / programmer in a quantitative biology lab at UNC Chapel Hill until the Fall of 2022. My lab studied sea turtle navigation and how by merely sensing Earth's magnetic field, turtle's can return to the exact beach they were born on - a fascinating ability called magnetoreception. Magnetoreception is exhibited in many different animals and most likely varies in mechanism depending on the species involved. In the case of sea turtles, my lab wished to elucidate the "how" they do it by implementing navigational algorithms in both simulations and robots. In the case of the latter, it was my job to design a robot platform that could be easily programmed by a user whom wishes to test their navigational algorithm.
